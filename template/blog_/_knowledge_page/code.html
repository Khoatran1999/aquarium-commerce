Neon Tetra Care Guide: The Jewel of the Aquarium

person
Dr. Sarah Fin
Updated Oct 24, 2023

Neon Tetras are among the most popular freshwater tropical fish. Their striking blue and red stripes make them a dazzling addition to any community tank. However, keeping them vibrant requires specific water parameters.

A school of Neon Tetras thriving in a planted environment.

Ideal Water Parameters

Neons originate from the blackwater streams of South America, which are typically soft and acidic. Replicating this environment is key to their longevity.

tips_and_updates

Pro-Tip: Stability is Key

Neon Tetras are sensitive to changes. Avoid large water changes (more than 30%) at once. Instead, opt for smaller, weekly changes to keep parameters stable.

Tank Mates

Because of their small size, Neons should only be kept with peaceful community fish. Avoid large or aggressive species like Cichlids or Angelfish that might see them as a snack.

  • Good Mates: Guppies, Rasboras, Corydoras Catfish.
  • Bad Mates: Goldfish, Oscars, Bettas (sometimes).
